Why You're Getting No Matches (And How to Fix It)

Published on January 2024

You've been swiping for weeks. Maybe months. Your thumb is getting a workout, but your inbox? Crickets. If you're wondering why you're getting no matches on dating apps, you're not alone. And more importantly, it's almost certainly fixable.

The truth is, most people make the same handful of mistakes that tank their match rate. Let's break down exactly what's going wrong and how to fix it.

Your First Photo Is Working Against You

Your first photo is everything. People spend less than one second deciding whether to swipe left or right. If your main photo doesn't immediately convey "trustworthy, attractive, and interesting," you're done.

Common first photo mistakes:

  • Group photos — Nobody wants to play "Where's Waldo" to figure out which one is you
  • Sunglasses or hats — People want to see your face, not your accessories
  • Low-quality or dark photos — If they can't see you clearly, they'll assume you're hiding something
  • Mirror selfies — These scream low effort and have been shown to significantly reduce match rates

The fix: Use a clear, well-lit solo photo where you're smiling naturally. Outdoor lighting works best. Have a friend take it — photos taken by others consistently outperform selfies.

Your Bio Says Nothing (Or Too Much)

A blank bio signals low effort. A novel-length bio signals desperation. The sweet spot? 2-3 punchy lines that give people something to talk about.

Bad bios include:

  • Generic statements like "I love to laugh" (who doesn't?)
  • Lists of demands ("Don't message me if...")
  • Self-deprecating humor that reads as insecurity
  • Nothing at all

You're Not Showing Range

If all your photos look the same — same angle, same setting, same expression — you're not giving people enough reasons to swipe right. A good profile tells a story.

Your photos should include:

  • A clear face shot (your main photo)
  • A full-body photo (so people know what to expect)
  • An action shot doing something you enjoy
  • A social photo (one with friends, not a group photo as your main)

The Algorithm Might Be Working Against You

Dating apps use algorithms to decide who sees your profile. If you've been swiping right on everyone, the algorithm assumes you're not selective and lowers your visibility. If you created your account long ago and haven't updated it, you've lost your "new user boost."

Quick fixes:

  • Be more selective with your swipes — only swipe right on people you'd actually want to match with
  • Update your profile regularly — new photos signal active engagement
  • Complete your profile fully — apps reward completed profiles with more visibility

The Bottom Line

Getting no matches isn't a reflection of your worth as a person. It's almost always a fixable profile problem. Start with your first photo, tighten up your bio, and make sure you're showing enough variety to pique interest.
